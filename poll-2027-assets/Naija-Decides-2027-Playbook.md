# Naija Decides 2027 — Poll Launch Playbook

A complete guide to running your 2027 presidential straw poll on **vote.torama.money**:
branding, exact setup steps, ready-to-paste candidate bios, and a social plan to
push for reach. Built around your app's open-link voting, photo + bio cards, and
the live animated results board.

> **Read this first — keep it credible.** An open online poll is an *informal
> straw poll*, not a scientific survey, and not an official result. It can be
> influenced by who shares it and by people voting more than once (incognito
> windows, multiple phones, VPNs). Label it honestly everywhere as an **informal
> opinion poll, one vote per device, not affiliated with INEC or any government
> body.** That honesty is also what protects you and makes people trust the
> numbers enough to share them.

## 2. Set up the poll in the app — step by step

1. **Sign in** at `vote.torama.money` → *Create an election* (you'll get a
   passwordless email link).
2. **Title:** `Naija Decides 2027 — Who's Your President?`
   **Question/description:** `If the 2027 presidential election were held today, who would you vote for?`
3. **Ballot type:** *Single choice* (one pick per voter).
4. **Access mode:** *Open link — one vote per device.* This is the right mode for a
   public, viral poll: anyone with the link can vote, no codes needed, and each
   device is limited to one ballot.
5. **Add the three candidates** in this order (alphabetical = neutral). Upload each
   photo and paste the matching bio from section 3:
   - Atiku Abubakar
   - Peter Obi
   - Bola Ahmed Tinubu
6. **Upload the logo** (`logo.png`) as the election logo.
7. **Results visibility:** set results to **public / show after voting**, so each
   voter sees the live animated leaderboard the instant they submit. (That "watch
   it update live" moment is your single biggest sharing hook.)
8. **Schedule (optional):** set an open and close date/time in WAT — e.g. run it for
   2–4 weeks. A visible deadline ("voting closes 30 June") drives urgency. Or leave
   it open-ended.
9. **Open the election** and copy the share link.

Tip: keep candidate photos a **consistent size and framing** (head-and-shoulders,
similar zoom) so the ballot looks balanced and fair.

## 5. How to actually make it spread

- **Lead with the live results.** "Vote and watch it move in real time" beats "take
  a survey." Post a short screen-recording of the animated leaderboard updating.
- **WhatsApp is king in Nigeria.** Seed it into active group chats and Status, and
  ask each sharer to forward to 5 people. Most of your traffic will come from here,
  not X.
- **Post a daily standings update.** Screenshot the leaderboard each evening with
  "Day 3 standings 👇 — is your candidate winning? Vote: LINK." Recurring content
  keeps the poll in the timeline and pulls repeat shares.
- **Tag the energy, not the candidates' camps.** Engage hashtag communities
  (#Obidients, APC and ADC supporter tags) evenly so all sides feel invited — a poll
  only goes viral when every camp wants to prove their lead.
- **Pin it.** Pin the launch tweet and put the link in your bio/Linktree.
- **Micro-influencers > one big name.** Ten accounts with 5–50k engaged followers
  in Nigerian-politics Twitter will out-perform one celebrity repost.
- **Set a deadline and a finale.** "Voting closes Sunday 8pm — final result live."
  Endings create a second wave of urgency and a shareable conclusion.
- **Stay scrupulously neutral.** The moment the poll looks rigged for one side, the
  others stop sharing and it dies. Equal framing is also what keeps it spreading.
